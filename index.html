Activities

The one with the Google Developer Group Hackathon

Hackathons are the best, aren’t they? You get to meet a lot of interesting people who are equally crazy about tech as you are, and are willing to sacrifice a night’s sleep for hacking together a piece of software in under 24 hours just for fun (and of course a Google Home Mini). Let me share one such experience.

The hackathon was organized by Google Developer Group – Bay Area and held at 42 (an elite training institute focused towards creating great programmers) in Fremont, California.

What we built:

A inventory sharing system which allowed two or more merchants to access each other’s inventory right from the checkout/payment terminal and enabled them to sell the other merchant’s inventory items if they do not have those items themselves for a small commission. This system allowed merchants to make a profit off other merchant’s products in exchange for the customer referral.

We had to use Clover’s platform for payment endpoints which included Android-based handheld terminals. I was the only Android programmer on the team, and thus I took the responsibility of developing the Android application part that would be running on the Android-based terminal. My brilliant teammates took care of the back-end part since they were efficient at doing that.

We used Android, MongoDB, JavaScript, AWS, CollabEdit, and NodeJS to create the required APIs and backend framework required for communication with the Android application.

It was a day well spent even though we managed the 3rd prize and could not win the Google Home Mini.

P.S. I came home and ordered one from Google Store :)
